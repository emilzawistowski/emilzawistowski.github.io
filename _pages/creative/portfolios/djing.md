## Technical Skills

### Mixing Techniques
- **Beatmatching by ear** — Minimal sync dependency, traditional skills
- **EQ-focused blending** — 4-band isolator techniques
- **Long blends** — Extended transitions, 3-4 tracks layered
- **Filter work** — Resonance and frequency sweeps for tension
- **Effect use** — Space echo, reverb, delay for transitions

### Equipment Proficiency

**Preferred Setup:**
- **Pioneer DJM-V10** mixer (6-channel, 4-band EQ preferred)
- **Technics SL-1210** turntables

**Can work with:**
- DJM-900NXS2, Allen & Heath mixers
- CDJ-2000NXS2, XDJ-XZ
- Traktor, Serato, Rekordbox
- All-vinyl setups

## Set Preparation & Approach

### Preparation
- **No fully pre-planned sets** — Responsive to crowd and environment
- **Energy mapping** — Understanding venue, time slot, previous DJ
- **Music selection** — Bringing 2-3x more music than set length
- **Technical preparation** — USB backup, organized crates, tested tracks

### In the Booth
- **Read the room** — Adjusting to energy, crowd response, vibe
- **Build narrative** — Creating journey and arc across set duration
- **Stay present** — Not just executing a plan, but reacting and flowing
- **Technical precision** — Clean mixing, maintaining momentum

### Slot Adaptability

**Warm-up (early evening):**
- Lower BPM (120-125)
- Groovy, hypnotic selections
- Building foundation for night

**Prime time (midnight-03:00):**
- Higher energy (128-133 BPM)
- Driving, powerful tracks
- Peak moments and releases

**Late/early (03:00-09:00):**
- Hypnotic, deep selections
- Longer tracks, minimal progression
- Supporting marathon dancing

**Closing:**
- Coming down gradually
- Emotional, reflective tracks
- Leaving lasting impression
